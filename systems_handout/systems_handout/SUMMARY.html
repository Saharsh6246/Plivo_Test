Flaky Network: Low-Latency FEC Transport

The source creates a 160-byte audio frame every 20 ms. The sender forwards each frame immediately across UDP, while preserving its sequence number. For 24 of every 25 frames it also sends a compact XOR parity packet containing the current payload XOR the preceding payload.

The hostile relay can drop, delay, reorder, and duplicate any packet. The receiver stores payloads and parity by sequence number. A received parity plus either adjacent payload reconstructs the missing neighbor immediately; repeated local recovery also heals short runs of losses. The receiver then reconstructs the harness packet format and forwards it to the player.

This choice avoids both retransmission and block-formation latency. A NACK needs two impaired relay crossings, while block FEC waits for future source frames. The causal parity packet is transmitted with the protected frame, allowing the receiver to start recovery after just one relay crossing.

The protocol sends 164 bytes for every media frame and 162 bytes for 24 parity packets per 25-frame period: 1.9975x raw-stream bandwidth, below the 2.0x cap. The selected 80 ms playout delay was valid on both practice profiles and across Profile B seeds 1–5, with a worst measured miss rate of 0.80%.
